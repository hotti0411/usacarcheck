## 게시글 1 — r/tax (정보 PSA 각도)
**Title:** PSA: the new car-loan-interest deduction depends on your VIN, not the car's brand

```
Most coverage of the new $10k/yr car loan interest deduction skips the part that actually
decides if you qualify. Short version:

• Deduct up to $10,000/yr of interest on a NEW car loan, tax years 2025–2028.
• It's above-the-line — you do NOT have to itemize to claim it.
• THE CATCH: the vehicle must have had its FINAL ASSEMBLY in the U.S. "American brand"
  means nothing — assembly is plant-specific and lives on the VIN. A Toyota built in
  Kentucky can qualify; a Ford built in Mexico may not.
• Also required: new vehicle, first owner, personal use, loan after Dec 31 2024.
• Phases out above $100k MAGI ($200k joint).

To check: decode your VIN on NHTSA's free vPIC site and read "Plant Country." I got tired
of doing that by hand so I built a free one-step checker: usacarcheck.com (paste VIN →
yes/no). No signup, nothing stored. Mods — happy to pull the link, the info stands alone.
```

## 게시글 2 — r/whatcarshouldIbuy (구매 결정 각도)
**Title:** Buying new in 2025? The loan-interest deduction can swing your pick — but it's VIN-dependent

```
Something I wish more buyers knew before signing: the new car-loan-interest deduction
(up to $10k/yr through 2028) only applies if that specific vehicle's final assembly was
in the U.S. Two cars that look identical on the lot can differ because they rolled out of
different plants — even same make, same model, same year.

So "is it assembled in the US?" is now a real spec to check alongside MPG and price. The
data is on the VIN (NHTSA's "Plant Country" field). I built a free checker because the
only things ranking on Google were dealer pages with no actual tool: usacarcheck.com —
paste the VIN, get a yes/no plus the full requirement list.
```

# 💬 댓글 10개 (서로 다른 포인트 · 관련 질문에 답변으로)

검색 키워드: `car loan interest deduction`, `auto loan interest 2025 tax`,
`one big beautiful bill car`, `is my car eligible deduction`, `final assembly USA car`

**1. 브랜드≠조립지 (가장 핵심 오해)**
```
Important nuance: it's not about the brand being "American." It's about FINAL ASSEMBLY in
the U.S., which is plant-specific and on the VIN. Plenty of foreign brands assemble in the
U.S. and qualify; plenty of domestic brands assemble in Mexico/Canada and don't. Check the
actual VIN, not the logo. (Free checker if useful: usacarcheck.com)
```

**2. 확인 방법 (How-to)**
```
Easiest way to verify: decode your VIN on NHTSA's free vPIC database and look at the
"Plant Country" field — if it says United States, you're good on the assembly requirement.
I wrapped that into a one-click tool here so you skip the raw decoder: usacarcheck.com
```

**3. 소득 페이즈아웃**
```
One thing people miss: it phases out above $100k MAGI for single filers and $200k for
joint. So high earners may get a reduced deduction or none. The $10k cap is the interest
amount, not the deduction value.
```

**4. 중고차 제외**
```
Worth flagging — this is NEW vehicles only, and you have to be the first owner. Used cars
(even certified pre-owned) don't qualify, regardless of where they were assembled.
```

**5. 대출 시점 조건**
```
Also a timing requirement that catches people: the loan has to have originated after
Dec 31, 2024. Refinancing an older loan into 2025 doesn't make a pre-2025 purchase eligible.
```

**6. 리스 제외**
```
Quick correction since it comes up a lot: this is for purchase loans. Leases don't qualify —
there's no loan interest you're paying as the owner. Has to be a financed purchase.
```

**7. 구체 예시**
```
Concrete example of how the brand assumption fails: many Toyota Camrys are assembled in
Georgetown, KY (qualifies), while some Chevy/Ford models are assembled in Mexico (don't).
Same idea across brands — the plant is what matters. The VIN tells you which plant.
```

**8. above-the-line (항목별공제 불필요)**
```
Good news that's underreported: this is an above-the-line deduction, so you can take it
even if you take the standard deduction. You don't have to itemize for this one.
```

**9. $10k 상한 현실 체크**
```
Reality check on the $10k cap: that's the interest, not the loan. To pay $10k of interest
in a year you'd need a pretty large balance at current rates, so most buyers will deduct
well under the cap. Still real money, just set expectations.
```

**10. 오정보 정정형 (누가 틀린 말 했을 때)**
```
That's a common misconception — it isn't based on the manufacturer's country or the brand.
The statute keys off the vehicle's final assembly location, which is determined per-VIN
(NHTSA's data). Same model can qualify or not depending on the assembly plant. Easy to
verify the exact VIN: usacarcheck.com
```
